# Design Direction

## Scene

A fantasy reader visits at night from a laptop or phone, checking for the next volume after finishing a chapter. The site should feel like a candlelit archive, a fan forum sidebar, and a polished author hub sharing the same room.

## Color Strategy

Full palette. Use tinted near-black and umber surfaces, parchment panels, aged gold accents, muted crimson marks, and pale blue links. Colors should be defined in OKLCH where possible and avoid pure black or pure white.

## Typography

Use a readable sans body paired with a chunky display face for headings. Headings can feel carved or poster-like, but paragraph text must remain comfortable for long reading.

## Components

- Boxed shell with decorative double borders.
- Retro navigation badges and external-link chips.
- Reusable book cards with cover placeholders.
- Progress tracker rows with labeled meters and phases.
- Gallery tiles with tabs for fanart and official artwork.
- Newsletter and contact forms with success and error states.

## Texture

Use CSS-only parchment, stone, and starry/noisy texture effects. Do not rely on copyrighted fantasy art. Placeholder images should be clearly replaceable.
